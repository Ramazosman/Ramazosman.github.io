Sudanese Basta

History

Sudanese people bastta or backlava in many diffrent types of events like family gatherings to wedding or even funrals no matter the event this dessert always hase to be there.Around the eighth century B.C.E., people in the Assyrian Empire, which spread across parts of modern-day Iraq, Iran, Kuwait, Syria and Turkey, arranged unleavened flatbreads in layers, with chopped nuts in between. This has spread to North Africa and now there are many diffrent varinations like the sudanes vertions.The earliest versions of baklava as we know it today came around 500 years ago, during the Ottoman Empire.

This recipe gives you the similplest vertion of Basta the one most people enjoy without all the complecated

Ingredients

  • ½ package Phyllo Sheets
  • 1 cup ghee
  • 2 cups crushed peanuts (opitnal)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • ½ cup water

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degress Fahrenheit/ 176 degrees Celsius
  2. Lay out a filo pastry sheet and brush it with ghee using the pastry brush
  3. Lay another sheet on top of it and brush it with ghee countiue uunit used about ten sheets
  4. roll up the ten filo sheets together loosely to creat a long log place on a long baking pan
  5. Create more logs using the rest of the filo sheets and place the rest of the "logs" on the baking pan
  6. Place in the oven for about ten to 15 minutes or until golden brown
  7. In the mean time create the syrup using two cups of sugar
  8. Place the sugar in a por with 1/2 cups of water and stir slowly until the sugar has fully
  9. Water and sugar mixture should be clear
  10. After 15 to 20 miuntes the pastries should be golden brown take them out of the oven and let them cool down for 10 minutes
  11. After 10 miuntes pass cut the pastries into small logs that should be no longer than 3 inches
  12. After all the pasteries have been cut pour the syrup you have created over the pastries and let the syrup sink in
  13. for the best result wait over night
